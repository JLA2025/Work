Why Do We Follow Laws?

Political Obligation

This is when people feel it's their duty to follow laws and support the government because they believe it's the right thing to do.

Blind Obedience

This is when people follow orders without thinking about whether they're right or wrong.

Key Difference: Obligation = moral duty to do something | Obedience = just following orders

What If Laws Are Unfair?

Sometimes laws are unjust or governments become tyrannical. When this happens, people might feel justified in fighting back through:

  • Peaceful protests
  • Civil disobedience (breaking unfair laws peacefully)
  • Legal challenges (using the court system)
Historical Examples:
• Martin Luther King Jr. - Fought racist laws through peaceful protests
• Mahatma Gandhi - Led peaceful resistance against unfair British taxes in India

Different Ideas About Why We Should Follow Laws

Social Contract Theory

People give up some freedoms to live in a stable society. If the government breaks this deal, we don't have to obey.

Utilitarian View

Laws exist to make the most people happy. We follow them because they create the greatest good.

Communitarian Approach

What's good for the community is more important than what individuals want.

Making Laws and Dealing with Crime

Types of Laws

  • Statutory laws - Made by parliaments and congresses
  • Common law - Develops from court decisions over time
  • Constitutional law - The basic rules that set up a country's legal system

What Is Crime?

Breaking laws that the state has defined and can punish you for. It's not just about what's morally wrong.

Why Do We Punish People?

  • Retribution - Payback for wrongdoing
  • Deterrent - Stop others from doing the same thing
  • Rehabilitation - Help criminals become better people

The Rule of Law

"No one is above the law"

What This Means:

  • Equality - Everyone follows the same laws, whether rich or poor
  • Fairness - Laws are applied the same way for everyone
  • Transparency - Laws are clear and public
  • Accountability - Even government officials must follow the law
  • Accessible Justice - Everyone can use the court system
Examples: A police officer getting a speeding ticket, being able to sue a big company, laws being available online, peaceful elections

Why the Rule of Law Is Important

Protection of Rights

Safeguards basic freedoms like speech and assembly

Economic Growth

Businesses invest more when laws are fair and predictable

Reduced Corruption

Clear rules stop officials from abusing their power

Stability

People can plan their lives when they know what to expect

Power: The Engine of Politics

Power is the ability to influence other people's behaviour or achieve what you want.

Coercive Power

Using threats or force
Example: Legal system, military sanctions

Reward Power

Offering benefits or incentives
Example: Tax breaks, campaign funding

Legitimate Power

Authority from your position
Example: Elected officials, judges

Informational Power

Controlling valuable knowledge
Example: Media outlets, experts

Usually, political power involves a combination of these types!

Political Systems Around the World

These define how power is gained, shared, and used in a society.

Democracy

Power belongs to the people, exercised through elected representatives

Monarchy

Power belongs to a hereditary ruler (king or queen)

Republic

Power belongs to the people but not exercised directly

Authoritarianism

Power concentrated in one leader or small group

Totalitarianism

The state controls all aspects of public and private life

Research Task: The State of Democracy

Choose a country and research how democratic it really is today.

What to Look For:

  • What type of political system does it have and how has it changed?
  • How much can citizens participate in politics?
  • What challenges face democratic institutions? (corruption, populism, etc.)
  • Where does the country rank in global democracy rankings?
Good Sources: Academic journals, government reports, think tank publications, credible news articles

Key Takeaways

  • People have a political obligation to follow laws, but this weakens when laws are unjust
  • The rule of law ensures everyone is treated equally
  • Power comes in many forms and shapes all political decisions
  • Different political systems organise power in different ways
  • Democracy faces ongoing challenges that require constant vigilance

Understanding these concepts helps us be better citizens and make informed decisions about our political systems.
