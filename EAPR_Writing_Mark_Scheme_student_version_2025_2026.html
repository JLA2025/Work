Student-friendly assessment grid: EAP Research project

Grade Task achievement and critical analysis Independent research and sources Organisation Academic conventions
80%+
Excellent		 Show original thinking:
  • Bring fresh insights and innovative ideas to sustainable living
  • Challenge assumptions and question commonly accepted ideas
  • Make sophisticated connections to broader academic concepts
  • Reveal new perspectives and understanding through creative analysis
 Go beyond basic sources:
  • Use creative research methods to find unique, high-quality materials
  • Create new insights by connecting sources in original ways
  • Show exceptional research independence
  • Develop independent arguments from source material
 Use innovative structure:
  • Organise your essay in ways that enhance your argument
  • Create sophisticated topic sentences with original connections
  • Go beyond conventional academic formats creatively
  • Build compelling, logical arguments through structure
 Master academic conventions:
  • Use academic language naturally and effectively
  • Integrate sources seamlessly into your writing
  • Show sophisticated citation skills that strengthen arguments
  • Demonstrate exceptional accuracy in referencing
70-79%
Very good		 Analyse effectively:
  • Use established methods to examine issues critically
  • Challenge some assumptions and evaluate different perspectives
  • Make clear connections to relevant academic disciplines
  • Show comprehensive understanding with clear insights
 Use strong analytical skills:
  • Evaluate sources systematically within academic frameworks
  • Synthesise sources effectively to support arguments
  • Show good research skills with appropriate academic sources
  • Develop well-supported arguments through effective integration
 Organise clearly:
  • Use conventional academic structures effectively
  • Write coherent paragraphs with clear topic sentences
  • Use good transitions to connect ideas smoothly
  • Show well-organised, coherent text throughout
 Use consistent academic tone:
  • Maintain appropriate register throughout
  • Reference sources correctly with only minor errors
  • Write clearly using academic language appropriately
  • Show strong citation and referencing skills
60-69%
Good		 Show good understanding:
  • Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of sustainable living issues
  • Develop critical thinking and begin to question assumptions
  • Make some connections to academic concepts (still developing)
  • Show evidence of independence in assessment
 Use good research strategies:
  • Find and evaluate sources with evidence of independence
  • Synthesise information systematically
  • Include mostly relevant sources with good evaluation
  • Show originality in research approach
 Organise information coherently:
  • Arrange ideas logically with clear progression
  • Write effective paragraphs with clear topic sentences
  • Use cohesive devices effectively throughout most of essay
  • Show variety in organisational approaches
 Generally use academic tone:
  • Maintain appropriate register with occasional lapses
  • Reference sources well but may have some inaccuracies
  • Use academic vocabulary appropriately
  • Show good citation attempts with minor errors
50-59%
Satisfactory		 Show basic understanding:
  • Demonstrate knowledge of key concepts with clear arguments
  • Use mainly description (try to analyse more!)
  • Make basic connections to academic concepts
  • Show little ability to challenge assumptions
 Use basic research strategies:
  • Find sources and attempt evaluation
  • Include fair range of sources with some evaluation attempts
  • Develop clear arguments, though analysis may be limited
  • Show basic attempts to relate sources to question
 Organise adequately:
  • Structure essay reasonably well, though may lack overall progression
  • Use topic sentences inconsistently
  • Use basic linking but may have gaps in coherence
  • Show some organisation but may over-use cohesive devices
 Attempt academic tone:
  • Try to use academic language but may have inappropriate language
  • Make citation attempts but may have errors or omissions
  • Show some academic awareness but inconsistently applied
  • Struggle with paraphrasing - may have weak attempts
40-49%
Below standard		 Show limited understanding:
  • Demonstrate basic knowledge but minimal connection to research question
  • Accept information uncritically - need to question more
  • Show minimal analysis - focus mainly on description
  • Show limited ability to challenge assumptions
 Use basic source identification:
  • Find some sources but with minimal evaluation
  • Include limited or unreliable sources
  • Develop basic arguments but lack detailed support
  • Show very little analysis with ideas difficult to identify
 Show some organisation:
  • Present information with basic structure but lacks clear progression
  • Use unclear or absent topic sentences
  • Overuse or underuse linking words inappropriately
  • Show limited effectiveness in paragraph development
 Show limited academic awareness:
  • Frequently use inappropriate language for academic context
  • Struggle with citations - significant errors or plagiarism issues
  • Need to develop academic conventions significantly
  • Show weak paraphrasing with significant lifting
20-40%
Poor		 Show very little understanding:
  • Demonstrate minimal grasp of main concepts
  • Lack critical thinking - unable to evaluate information
  • Miss research focus - content may be irrelevant
  • Show no clear disciplinary connection
 Use very limited sources:
  • Include few or no appropriate academic sources
  • Show no source evaluation - accept all information
  • Lack clear arguments - ideas difficult to identify
  • Show little or no analysis attempted
 Lack logical organisation:
  • Present information without clear structure
  • Use poor paragraph structure with no clear topic sentences
  • Use only basic connectors like "first," "then," "after"
  • Show no effective paragraph development
 Show no academic awareness:
  • Writing inappropriate for academic context
  • Lack citation skills - no proper referencing
  • Require fundamental improvement in academic writing
  • Show unacceptable academic style and conventions
0-20%
Unacceptable		 Show no understanding:
  • Content not relevant to research question
  • Lack any critical thinking or analytical engagement
  • Miss all academic requirements
  • Show no evidence of academic content
 Use no appropriate sources:
  • Show no evidence of research or source evaluation
  • Make no attempt to evaluate or synthesise information
  • Provide content barely related to the task
  • Show no clear position or argument
 Fail to organise:
  • Show no logical structure or clear progression
  • Lack paragraph skills - no effective structure
  • Use very limited linking words
  • Show no evidence of organisational planning
 Unacceptable for academic context:
  • Writing does not meet basic academic standards
  • Show no academic conventions or citation skills
  • Require complete redevelopment of academic writing
  • Need to start again with fundamental skills

Remember: The difference between grade levels often comes down to depth of analysis, originality of thinking, and sophistication of academic writing skills. Focus on developing these areas to improve your performance!
